    Problem Identification
    At approximately 13:05, a routine sensor alert indicated a 30% drop in hydraulic pressure in the HP-200 press unit. Upon physical inspection, maintenance staff identified a visible stream of oil seeping from the lower rear section of the actuator housing. The surrounding area showed light pooling and trace spray patterns on the machine frame, suggesting a pressurized leak.
    Leak site overview ‚Äì taken at 13:10
    The leak was active but not catastrophic, and the decision was made to proceed with a controlled shutdown to minimize further damage.
    Containment Measures
    Within 5 minutes of detection, the press was transitioned into safe shutdown mode via the local control panel. Oil collection mats were deployed and the leak area was wiped clean to allow visual inspection of the failure point. A temporary industrial-grade epoxy sealant was applied to the fitting as a preventive measure while sourcing the replacement part.
    Short clip showing fluid under pressure ‚Äì captured 13:11
    Root Cause Analysis
    Disassembly of the affected area revealed a crack in the metal coupling on the secondary hydraulic hose. Visual and tactile examination indicated fatigue-related stress fractures around the threads. Maintenance records from March and May 2025 had flagged early wear on similar components but no replacements had yet been scheduled.
    Close-up of cracked metal fitting ‚Äì documented during inspection
    This is now the third known failure of this hose type in 6 months, which indicates a recurring design or materials issue. Component sourcing will be reviewed.
    Corrective Action
    A new fitting was ordered from on-site inventory with expected delivery at 15:00. The technician cleaned and prepped the hose threads and actuator port for installation. All fittings within the same pressure line were visually inspected, and two additional fittings were marked for preventive replacement during the next scheduled maintenance window.
    Verification
    A check of vibration and temperature logs from the past 48 hours showed no external abnormalities that could have contributed to the failure. After installing the new part, the system was repressurized and monitored for 30 minutes. All pressure readings returned to nominal values with no visible fluid loss.
    Documentation & Follow-Up
    Maintenance records have been updated in the internal system, and this incident has been flagged for review during the next maintenance strategy review. The inspection protocol for HP-200 class presses was updated to include a monthly check of secondary hose couplings and a quarterly ultrasound inspection of metal fatigue points. A follow-up inspection has been scheduled for 21/7/2025 to verify the stability of the repaired section under full operational load.
